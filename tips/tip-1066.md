# TIP-1066: Deprecate TIP-20 Rewards

## Abstract

This TIP deprecates TIP-20 rewards at T6. After activation, new reward opt-ins and new reward distributions revert, and TIP-20 transfer, mint, and burn paths bypass rewards accounting. Already-accrued rewards remain claimable indefinitely through the existing claim mechanics.

## Motivation

TIP-20 rewards add accounting work to balance-changing TIP-20 paths even when rewards are no longer expected to be used. Deprecating rewards lets the protocol remove that hot-path work without introducing the `UserState` balance-bit optimization from the prior performance-optimization proposal.

This TIP supersedes and reverts that previous performance-optimization TIP. The intended performance outcome is the same: TIP-20 balance changes no longer need to perform rewards update checks after T6. The mechanism is simpler because rewards are disabled at the protocol level rather than bypassed conditionally through additional user state.

## Assumptions

- T6 is the activation version for this behavior.
- No new reward distributions are expected before or after T6 activation.
- Any rewards accrued before T6 remain part of canonical protocol state and must remain claimable.
- Existing rewards-related storage may remain allocated indefinitely.
- SDKs and client libraries can remove support for deprecated reward-management function selectors after T6 while retaining any claim surface needed for legacy accrued rewards.

If a concrete future customer need for TIP-20 rewards emerges, the path is a new TIP and design rather than re-enabling this deprecated mechanism.

## Threat Model

The relevant actors are:

- Token issuers and reward administrators, who may have previously configured TIP-20 rewards and attempted distributions.
- TIP-20 holders, who may have already-accrued claimable rewards.
- SDKs, indexers, and clients, which may still expose reward opt-in or distribution calls.

This TIP does not introduce new permissions or new reward authority. It removes the ability to create new rewards activity while preserving the ability for holders to claim balances that accrued before activation.

# Specification

The key words "MUST", "MUST NOT", "REQUIRED", "SHALL", "SHALL NOT", "SHOULD", "SHOULD NOT", "RECOMMENDED", "MAY", and "OPTIONAL" in this document are to be interpreted as described in RFC 2119.

## Activation

This TIP activates at T6.

Before T6, TIP-20 rewards behavior is unchanged.

At and after T6, TIP-20 rewards are disabled except for claims of already-accrued rewards.

## New opt-ins

At and after T6, reward opt-in entry points MUST revert.

The implementation MUST NOT create new opted-in reward state, update opted-in supply, or otherwise make an account newly eligible for TIP-20 rewards after T6.

## New distributions

At and after T6, reward distribution entry points, including `distributeRewards`, MUST revert.

The implementation MUST NOT create new reward distributions, update reward indexes for newly distributed amounts, or increase claimable reward balances through a post-T6 distribution.

## Existing opted-in state

At and after T6, existing opted-in state is ignored for all non-claim behavior.

In particular:

- opted-in status MUST NOT cause transfer, mint, or burn paths to perform rewards update checks
- opted-in status MUST NOT make an account eligible for newly distributed rewards
- opted-in supply MUST NOT be updated by transfer, mint, burn, opt-in, or distribution paths

Existing opted-in state may remain in storage, but it has no semantic effect after T6 except where the current claim implementation needs to read legacy state to preserve already-accrued claims.

## Transfer, mint, and burn behavior

At and after T6, TIP-20 transfer, mint, and burn paths MUST bypass TIP-20 rewards accounting.

These paths MUST NOT:

- update rewards for the sender
- update rewards for the recipient
- update rewards for the mint recipient or burn source
- update opted-in supply
- read or write rewards state solely to maintain post-T6 rewards eligibility

The TIP-20 balance and supply semantics unrelated to rewards are unchanged.

## Claims

Already-accrued rewards MUST remain claimable indefinitely.

Claim entry points MUST keep the current API and accounting semantics unless a later TIP explicitly changes them. Claiming MUST NOT accrue new rewards, create new distributions, or re-enable opted-in behavior.

The protocol MUST preserve enough rewards-related state to allow claims of rewards accrued before T6. No one-time storage cleanup or migration is required by this TIP.

## Storage

Rewards-related storage is left untouched unless it is updated by an allowed legacy claim.

The implementation MUST NOT perform a one-time cleanup, migration, or deletion of rewards state as part of T6 activation. Conceptually, rewards state is frozen at T6 except for claim debits and any existing claim-accounting writes required by the current claim mechanics.

## SDK and client behavior

SDKs SHOULD stop supporting deprecated reward opt-in and distribution function selectors after T6.

SDKs MAY retain claim helpers for already-accrued rewards. User interfaces SHOULD present rewards as disabled with claim-only backwards compatibility.

## Compatibility

This TIP is backwards compatible for holders with already-accrued rewards because claim mechanics are preserved indefinitely.

This TIP is intentionally not backwards compatible for callers that attempt to opt in to rewards or distribute rewards after T6. Those calls revert.

Indexers and clients should treat rewards as disabled at T6. Any existing opted-in state should be displayed, if at all, as ignored legacy state rather than active eligibility.

# Observability

No new events are required.

Existing revert behavior is sufficient for detecting post-T6 attempts to opt in or distribute rewards. Existing claim events, if any, remain the source of truth for legacy reward claims.

# Invariants

- At and after T6, no account can newly opt in to TIP-20 rewards.
- At and after T6, no new reward distribution can be created.
- At and after T6, TIP-20 transfer, mint, and burn paths do not update rewards accounting.
- At and after T6, existing opted-in state has no non-claim semantic effect.
- Already-accrued rewards remain claimable after T6.
- Claiming already-accrued rewards does not accrue new rewards or create a new distribution.
- Rewards-related storage is not cleaned up or migrated as part of T6 activation.
- This TIP supersedes the prior performance-optimization TIP that required `UserState`.
