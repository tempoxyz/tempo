# TIP-1080: Native Vault-Backed TIP-20 Tokens

## Abstract

This TIP extends TIP-20 with native vault-backed tokens whose token address is also the vault address. A vault-backed TIP-20 is a normal transferable TIP-20 balance for payments, fees, wallets, indexing, and policy checks, while deposits and withdrawals are handled by native vault logic at the same address.

The TIP defines two vault types. Single-strategy vaults route deposits directly into one configured strategy adapter. Multi-strategy vaults hold a native accounting surface and expose a constrained `manage` interface so an external manager, including a Veda-compatible manager adapter, can rebalance vault-owned liquidity without owning the user-facing deposit or withdrawal path.

The design keeps the useful vault-token separation from the earlier wrapper model: shares are TIP-20 balances, direct issuance is disabled, minting and burning only happen through vault accounting, and the factory registers vault shares as TIP-20 tokens. It changes the backing model from an external ERC-4626 vault to a native multi-asset vault designed around Tempo product requirements.

## Motivation

TIP-20 tokens are the native token surface for Tempo wallets, policy checks, indexing, payment lanes, and fee-token routing. Yield-bearing balances should use that same surface. Users should be able to receive, hold, transfer, approve, pay, and spend vault shares like ordinary TIP-20 assets, while still entering and exiting through vault-specific deposit and withdrawal methods.

An external ERC-4626 wrapper is too narrow for the target product. ERC-4626 assumes one underlying asset, with one `asset()` and asset-denominated deposit and withdrawal functions. Native Tempo vaults need multi-asset deposits, multi-asset withdrawals, policy checks on both sides of the flow, approve-less TIP-20 deposits through TIP-1035 where safe, optional fixed-yield accounting, and a manager interface for active allocation.

ERC-7575 is directionally closer because it separates share tokens from multiple vault entry points, but it is not adopted broadly enough to be the binding protocol abstraction. This TIP does not require ERC-4626 or ERC-7575 compliance. Implementations MAY expose compatibility adapters for integrators, but the native ABI is defined from first principles.

The product split is:

- Tempo owns the vault token, share accounting, user-facing deposits and withdrawals, policy checks, fee accounting, and native reward primitives.
- The vault owner controls configuration.
- The vault manager controls only managed execution for rebalancing vault-owned liquidity.
- External systems may provide strategy construction, curation UI, manager proof systems, Merkle root generation, reward campaign operations, and reporting.

## Assumptions

- Vault-backed TIP-20 shares are fungible. A vault therefore has one configured `DistributionMode`; users choose deposit and withdrawal assets, not a per-withdrawal yield formula.
- The vault address and the share token address are the same object and are registered as TIP-20 by the factory.
- The vault share token uses ordinary TIP-20 decimals, metadata, transfers, approvals, permits, memos, pause behavior, receive-policy behavior, and transfer-policy behavior, except that direct issuer mint and burn functions are disabled.
- Only vault deposit and withdrawal accounting can mint or burn vault shares.
- The vault has exactly two native control roles: `owner` and `manager`. The owner configures the vault. The manager executes constrained rebalancing for multi-strategy vaults.
- Multi-asset accounting is normalized through a configured `baseAsset`. The base asset is the accounting unit; it is not the only asset a vault can accept or return.
- Each non-base supported asset has an owner-configured rate provider or pricing source. Managers cannot set rate providers or prices.
- Synchronous withdrawals are the initial core path. Assets that cannot be delivered synchronously MUST report zero available liquidity through preview and max functions until enough withdrawal buffer is available. Async redemption can be added later as an additive extension.
- A vault-backed TIP-20 MAY be added to the TIP-1035 Implicit Approval List, but only for user-initiated deposit flows that pull the caller's tokens or use a non-replayable authorization from the token owner.
- A vault-backed TIP-20 MAY be added to the TIP-1045 payment call allow-list for bounded deposit and withdrawal selectors. `manage` and Merkle proof claim selectors are not payment-lane eligible.

## Threat Model

- **Vault owner:** trusted to configure supported assets, rate providers, policies, fees, distribution mode, strategy adapter, reward roots, manager address, and emergency pause state. A malicious or compromised owner can misconfigure the vault or route future accounting to bad configuration.
- **Vault manager:** trusted only to move vault-owned liquidity through `manage` on multi-strategy vaults. The manager MUST NOT be able to mint shares, burn shares, change owner configuration, update policies, update rates, pull assets from arbitrary users, or call user-facing vault methods reentrantly.
- **Single-strategy adapter:** trusted to interact with one yield source and report strategy value correctly. A malicious or buggy adapter can lose or misprice strategy assets for the vaults that use it.
- **Rate providers:** trusted to return fresh, normalized asset prices. Stale or manipulated rates can cause unfair share issuance, unfair withdrawal amounts, or incorrect fee accounting.
- **TIP-403 and receive-policy checks:** trusted to enforce issuer and address-level restrictions. Vault policy failures MUST happen before assets or shares move.
- **Reward root submitter:** in this TIP, the owner submits reward roots. Bad roots can allocate rewards incorrectly, but cannot mint shares or move principal except through funded reward balances.
- **Users and approved spenders:** may deposit supported assets, transfer shares, approve allowances, redeem shares, and claim rewards subject to policy checks, balances, allowances, and slippage bounds.
- **External curation systems:** may sit behind the manager address. They are not part of the trusted deposit or withdrawal path.

# Specification

## Overview

A vault-backed TIP-20 combines three surfaces at one address:

1. The ordinary TIP-20 share-token surface for balances, transfers, approvals, permits, memos, metadata, pause state, transfer policies, receive policies, and factory registration.
2. A native multi-asset vault surface for deposits, withdrawals, previews, accounting, fees, and reward claims.
3. For multi-strategy vaults only, a BoringVault-compatible `manage` surface for externally curated rebalancing.

The factory MUST register the created vault address as a TIP-20 token, so `ITIP20Factory.isTIP20(vault) == true`. It MUST also expose a vault-specific registry query, so contracts can distinguish ordinary TIP-20 tokens from native vault-backed TIP-20 tokens.

## Factory Interface

The existing TIP-20 factory is extended with native vault creation. A separate vault factory MAY be introduced instead if implementation boundaries require it, but the canonical created vault address MUST still be registered as a TIP-20 token.

```solidity
interface ITIP20VaultFactory {
    /// @notice Vault implementation type selected at creation.
    enum VaultType {
        /// @notice Deposits are routed into one immutable strategy adapter.
        SINGLE_STRATEGY,
        /// @notice A manager rebalances vault-owned liquidity through manage().
        MULTI_STRATEGY
    }

    /// @notice Vault-level yield and reward accounting mode.
    enum DistributionMode {
        /// @notice All yield remains in NAV and accrues pro rata to all shares.
        PRO_RATA,
        /// @notice Share value grows at a configured target rate.
        FIXED_YIELD,
        /// @notice Arbitrary rewards are claimed through cumulative Merkle proofs.
        MERKLE_REWARDS
    }

    /// @notice Initial fixed-yield configuration.
    struct FixedYieldConfig {
        /// @notice Target annualized user yield in basis points.
        uint64 annualRateBps;
        /// @notice Optional reserve used to cover shortfalls. Zero means no reserve.
        address reserve;
        /// @notice Recipient of yield above the fixed-yield liability.
        address excessRecipient;
        /// @notice If true, deposits and withdrawals pause when the target rate is underfunded.
        bool pauseOnShortfall;
    }

    /// @notice Initial fee configuration.
    struct FeeConfig {
        /// @notice Recipient for management and performance fees.
        address feeRecipient;
        /// @notice Annual management fee in basis points.
        uint16 managementFeeBps;
        /// @notice Performance fee in basis points of yield above the high-water mark.
        uint16 performanceFeeBps;
    }

    /// @notice Native vault initialization parameters.
    struct VaultInit {
        /// @notice TIP-20 share token name.
        string name;
        /// @notice TIP-20 share token symbol.
        string symbol;
        /// @notice ISO or issuer-defined currency string.
        string currency;
        /// @notice Quote token used by existing TIP-20 quote-token rules.
        address quoteToken;
        /// @notice Optional TIP-1026 logo URI.
        string logoURI;
        /// @notice Accounting asset used for NAV, fees, and share conversion.
        address baseAsset;
        /// @notice Initial owner. Must be nonzero.
        address owner;
        /// @notice Initial manager. Required for MULTI_STRATEGY, ignored for SINGLE_STRATEGY.
        address manager;
        /// @notice Single-strategy adapter. Required for SINGLE_STRATEGY, zero for MULTI_STRATEGY.
        address strategy;
        /// @notice Initial transfer policy governing share transfers and share mints.
        uint64 shareTransferPolicyId;
        /// @notice Vault implementation type.
        VaultType vaultType;
        /// @notice Initial vault-level distribution mode.
        DistributionMode distributionMode;
        /// @notice Initial fixed-yield config. Ignored unless distributionMode is FIXED_YIELD.
        FixedYieldConfig fixedYieldConfig;
        /// @notice Initial fee config.
        FeeConfig feeConfig;
    }

    /// @notice Emitted after a native vault-backed TIP-20 is created.
    /// @dev The factory MUST emit the ordinary TIP-20 TokenCreated event first.
    event VaultTokenCreated(
        address indexed vault,
        VaultType indexed vaultType,
        DistributionMode indexed distributionMode,
        address owner,
        address manager,
        address strategy,
        address baseAsset,
        uint64 shareTransferPolicyId,
        bytes32 salt
    );

    /// @notice Creates a native vault-backed TIP-20 token.
    /// @param init Vault initialization parameters.
    /// @param salt Deterministic address salt.
    /// @return vault The created vault and TIP-20 share token address.
    function createVaultToken(VaultInit calldata init, bytes32 salt) external returns (address vault);

    /// @notice Returns true if an address is an initialized native vault-backed TIP-20.
    /// @param vault Address to query.
    /// @return True if the address is a vault-backed TIP-20 created by this factory.
    function isVaultToken(address vault) external view returns (bool);

    /// @notice Predicts the address for createVaultToken.
    /// @param sender The account that will call createVaultToken.
    /// @param salt The deterministic address salt.
    /// @return vault The predicted vault address.
    function getVaultTokenAddress(address sender, bytes32 salt) external pure returns (address vault);
}
```

`createVaultToken` MUST:

1. Validate `owner != address(0)`.
2. Validate `baseAsset` and `quoteToken` using the same TIP-20 factory rules that apply to ordinary TIP-20 tokens, unless a future asset-onboarding TIP explicitly allows non-TIP-20 assets.
3. Validate `shareTransferPolicyId` using the same TIP-403 policy existence rules that apply to ordinary TIP-20 token policy configuration.
4. Validate `logoURI` using TIP-1026 rules if non-empty.
5. For `SINGLE_STRATEGY`, validate `strategy != address(0)` and `manager == address(0)`.
6. For `MULTI_STRATEGY`, validate `manager != address(0)` and `strategy == address(0)`.
7. Validate fee and fixed-yield configuration, including nonzero recipients whenever the corresponding fee or excess path can accrue.
8. Deploy or initialize the vault-backed TIP-20 at the deterministic address.
9. Register the created address as a TIP-20 token and as a native vault token.
10. Emit the existing TIP-20 `TokenCreated` event, then emit `VaultTokenCreated`.

The created vault MUST NOT grant any role or capability that can mint or burn shares outside the vault deposit and withdrawal flows.

## Vault Interface

The vault interface below defines the additional selectors exposed by a vault-backed TIP-20. The same address MUST also support ordinary TIP-20 selectors, except where this TIP overrides direct issuance and direct burn behavior.

```solidity
interface INativeTIP20Vault {
    enum VaultType {
        SINGLE_STRATEGY,
        MULTI_STRATEGY
    }

    enum DistributionMode {
        PRO_RATA,
        FIXED_YIELD,
        MERKLE_REWARDS
    }

    struct AssetConfig {
        /// @notice Whether this asset may be deposited by users.
        bool canDeposit;
        /// @notice Whether this asset may be withdrawn by users.
        bool canWithdraw;
        /// @notice Whether this asset is the base accounting asset.
        bool isBaseAsset;
        /// @notice Rate provider used to normalize this asset into baseAsset. Zero for baseAsset.
        address rateProvider;
        /// @notice TIP-403 policy checked before deposits of this asset.
        uint64 depositPolicyId;
        /// @notice TIP-403 policy checked before withdrawals of this asset.
        uint64 withdrawalPolicyId;
        /// @notice Maximum total deposited amount for this asset. Zero means uncapped.
        uint128 depositCap;
        /// @notice Target liquid balance for synchronous withdrawals.
        uint128 withdrawalBufferTarget;
    }

    struct FeeConfig {
        address feeRecipient;
        uint16 managementFeeBps;
        uint16 performanceFeeBps;
    }

    struct FixedYieldConfig {
        uint64 annualRateBps;
        address reserve;
        address excessRecipient;
        bool pauseOnShortfall;
    }

    struct RewardProgram {
        /// @notice Current cumulative-claim Merkle root.
        bytes32 root;
        /// @notice Claims before this timestamp revert.
        uint64 validAfter;
        /// @notice Whether the program accepts claims.
        bool active;
    }

    struct RewardClaim {
        /// @notice Reward program identifier.
        bytes32 programId;
        /// @notice Account whose cumulative allocation is proven.
        address account;
        /// @notice Reward asset transferred to receiver.
        address rewardAsset;
        /// @notice Total cumulative amount allocated to account.
        uint256 cumulativeAmount;
        /// @notice Merkle proof for the domain-separated cumulative leaf.
        bytes32[] proof;
    }

    /// @notice Emitted after a successful deposit into the vault.
    event Deposit(
        address indexed caller,
        address indexed owner,
        address indexed asset,
        uint256 assets,
        uint256 shares,
        bytes32 memo
    );

    /// @notice Emitted after a successful withdrawal or redemption from the vault.
    event Withdraw(
        address indexed caller,
        address indexed receiver,
        address indexed owner,
        address asset,
        uint256 assets,
        uint256 shares,
        bytes32 memo
    );

    event AssetConfigUpdated(address indexed asset, AssetConfig config);
    event FeeConfigUpdated(FeeConfig config);
    event FixedYieldConfigUpdated(FixedYieldConfig config);
    event DistributionModeUpdated(DistributionMode indexed mode);
    event ManagerUpdated(address indexed oldManager, address indexed newManager);
    event OwnerTransferStarted(address indexed oldOwner, address indexed pendingOwner);
    event OwnerUpdated(address indexed oldOwner, address indexed newOwner);
    event RewardProgramUpdated(bytes32 indexed programId, bytes32 root, uint64 validAfter, bool active);
    event RewardClaimed(
        bytes32 indexed programId,
        address indexed account,
        address indexed rewardAsset,
        address receiver,
        uint256 amount
    );
    event Managed(address indexed manager, address indexed target, uint256 value, bytes4 selector);
    event BatchManaged(address indexed manager, uint256 calls);

    /// @notice Returns the factory that created this vault.
    function factory() external view returns (address);

    /// @notice Returns the vault type selected at creation.
    function vaultType() external view returns (VaultType);

    /// @notice Returns the current vault owner.
    function owner() external view returns (address);

    /// @notice Returns the pending owner set through two-step ownership transfer.
    function pendingOwner() external view returns (address);

    /// @notice Returns the manager allowed to call manage on multi-strategy vaults.
    function manager() external view returns (address);

    /// @notice Returns the single-strategy adapter, or zero for multi-strategy vaults.
    function strategy() external view returns (address);

    /// @notice Returns the accounting asset used for NAV and share conversion.
    function baseAsset() external view returns (address);

    /// @notice Returns the current vault-level distribution mode.
    function distributionMode() external view returns (DistributionMode);

    /// @notice Returns the per-asset vault configuration.
    function assetConfig(address asset) external view returns (AssetConfig memory);

    /// @notice Returns the current fee configuration.
    function feeConfig() external view returns (FeeConfig memory);

    /// @notice Returns the current fixed-yield configuration.
    function fixedYieldConfig() external view returns (FixedYieldConfig memory);

    /// @notice Returns the current reward program configuration.
    function rewardProgram(bytes32 programId) external view returns (RewardProgram memory);

    /// @notice Returns the cumulative amount already claimed for a reward tuple.
    function claimedReward(bytes32 programId, address account, address rewardAsset)
        external
        view
        returns (uint256 amount);

    /// @notice Returns whether an asset is currently deposit-enabled.
    function isDepositAsset(address asset) external view returns (bool);

    /// @notice Returns whether an asset is currently withdrawal-enabled.
    function isWithdrawalAsset(address asset) external view returns (bool);

    /// @notice Returns the vault's total holdings denominated in one supported asset.
    function totalAssets(address asset) external view returns (uint256 assets);

    /// @notice Returns total vault NAV in baseAsset units, net of accrued fees.
    function totalBaseAssets() external view returns (uint256 baseAssets);

    /// @notice Converts an asset amount to shares using current vault accounting.
    function convertToShares(address asset, uint256 assets) external view returns (uint256 shares);

    /// @notice Converts shares to an asset amount using current vault accounting.
    function convertToAssets(address asset, uint256 shares) external view returns (uint256 assets);

    /// @notice Returns the maximum asset amount receiver can deposit now.
    function maxDeposit(address asset, address receiver) external view returns (uint256 assets);

    /// @notice Returns the maximum shares receiver can mint through depositForShares now.
    function maxDepositForShares(address asset, address receiver) external view returns (uint256 shares);

    /// @notice Returns the maximum asset amount owner can withdraw now.
    function maxWithdraw(address asset, address owner) external view returns (uint256 assets);

    /// @notice Returns the maximum share amount owner can redeem now.
    function maxRedeem(address asset, address owner) external view returns (uint256 shares);

    /// @notice Previews a deposit of an exact asset amount.
    function previewDeposit(address asset, uint256 assets, address receiver)
        external
        view
        returns (uint256 shares);

    /// @notice Previews the asset amount needed to mint an exact share amount.
    function previewDepositForShares(address asset, uint256 shares, address receiver)
        external
        view
        returns (uint256 assets);

    /// @notice Previews the shares needed to withdraw an exact asset amount.
    function previewWithdraw(address asset, uint256 assets, address owner) external view returns (uint256 shares);

    /// @notice Previews the asset amount returned for redeeming an exact share amount.
    function previewRedeem(address asset, uint256 shares, address owner) external view returns (uint256 assets);

    /// @notice Deposits an exact amount of one supported asset and mints vault shares.
    function deposit(address asset, uint256 assets, address receiver) external returns (uint256 shares);

    /// @notice Deposits an exact amount with a memo and mints vault shares.
    function depositWithMemo(address asset, uint256 assets, address receiver, bytes32 memo)
        external
        returns (uint256 shares);

    /// @notice Deposits up to maxAssets to mint an exact share amount.
    function depositForShares(address asset, uint256 shares, uint256 maxAssets, address receiver)
        external
        returns (uint256 assets);

    /// @notice Burns up to maxShares to withdraw an exact amount of one supported asset.
    function withdraw(address asset, uint256 assets, uint256 maxShares, address receiver, address owner)
        external
        returns (uint256 shares);

    /// @notice Burns up to maxShares to withdraw an exact amount with a memo.
    function withdrawWithMemo(
        address asset,
        uint256 assets,
        uint256 maxShares,
        address receiver,
        address owner,
        bytes32 memo
    ) external returns (uint256 shares);

    /// @notice Burns an exact share amount and returns at least minAssets of one supported asset.
    function redeem(address asset, uint256 shares, uint256 minAssets, address receiver, address owner)
        external
        returns (uint256 assets);

    /// @notice Claims one or more cumulative Merkle reward allocations.
    function claimRewards(RewardClaim[] calldata claims, address receiver)
        external
        returns (uint256[] memory amounts);

    /// @notice Sets a pending owner. Callable only by owner.
    function setPendingOwner(address newPendingOwner) external;

    /// @notice Accepts ownership. Callable only by pendingOwner.
    function acceptOwner() external;

    /// @notice Updates the manager. Callable only by owner.
    function setManager(address newManager) external;

    /// @notice Updates per-asset configuration. Callable only by owner.
    function setAssetConfig(address asset, AssetConfig calldata config) external;

    /// @notice Updates fee configuration. Callable only by owner.
    function setFeeConfig(FeeConfig calldata config) external;

    /// @notice Updates the vault-level distribution mode. Callable only by owner.
    function setDistributionMode(DistributionMode mode) external;

    /// @notice Updates fixed-yield configuration. Callable only by owner.
    function setFixedYieldConfig(FixedYieldConfig calldata config) external;

    /// @notice Updates or disables a cumulative Merkle reward program. Callable only by owner.
    function updateRewardProgram(bytes32 programId, bytes32 root, uint64 validAfter, bool active) external;

    /// @notice Executes one manager call from a multi-strategy vault.
    function manage(address target, bytes calldata data, uint256 value) external returns (bytes memory result);

    /// @notice Executes an atomic batch of manager calls from a multi-strategy vault.
    function manage(address[] calldata targets, bytes[] calldata data, uint256[] calldata values)
        external
        returns (bytes[] memory results);
}
```

## TIP-20 Share Behavior

A vault-backed TIP-20 MUST support ordinary TIP-20 metadata, balances, transfers, approvals, permits, memos, pause state, logo URI, `quoteToken`, `currency`, transfer-policy, and receive-policy behavior.

The following ordinary TIP-20 functions MUST revert on a vault-backed TIP-20 because they would bypass vault accounting:

- `mint(address,uint256)`
- `mintWithMemo(address,uint256,bytes32)`
- `burn(uint256)`
- `burnWithMemo(uint256,bytes32)`
- `burnBlocked(address,uint256)`
- `distributeReward(uint256)`
- `claimRewards()` from the ordinary TIP-20 reward surface, if present

Vault rewards are handled by the vault-specific `claimRewards(RewardClaim[],address)` function. Share minting and burning remain available only through `deposit`, `depositWithMemo`, `depositForShares`, `withdraw`, `withdrawWithMemo`, and `redeem`.

The vault owner controls configuration. Implementations MAY expose existing TIP-20 role-management selectors for compatibility, but no role assignment may create a third native control role or enable direct share issuance. Any pause or unpause capability exposed through existing TIP-20 role selectors MUST be equivalent to owner-authorized emergency control.

## Asset Accounting

Each vault has one `baseAsset`. `baseAsset` is the accounting unit for NAV, shares, management fees, performance fees, and fixed-yield liabilities. It is not a restriction on deposit or withdrawal assets.

For every supported asset:

- `canDeposit` controls whether users can deposit it.
- `canWithdraw` controls whether users can receive it on withdrawal.
- `rateProvider` normalizes it into `baseAsset`, except that `baseAsset` MAY use `address(0)`.
- `depositPolicyId` gates deposit eligibility.
- `withdrawalPolicyId` gates withdrawal eligibility.
- `depositCap` constrains total deposits of that asset if nonzero.
- `withdrawalBufferTarget` describes desired liquid inventory for synchronous withdrawals.

Rate providers MUST expose:

```solidity
interface ITempoVaultRateProvider {
    /// @notice Returns the price of asset in baseAsset terms.
    /// @return rateRay The rate scaled by 1e27.
    /// @return updatedAt Timestamp of the price observation.
    function rate(address asset, address baseAsset)
        external
        view
        returns (uint256 rateRay, uint64 updatedAt);
}
```

The vault MUST reject stale or invalid rates before using them for user deposits, withdrawals, previews, fees, or fixed-yield accounting. The owner configures rate providers. The manager cannot configure rate providers or otherwise define user-facing asset prices.

`totalBaseAssets()` MUST return NAV net of accrued fees according to the configured distribution mode. `totalAssets(asset)` MUST convert the current base NAV into the requested supported asset using the configured rate provider. Withdrawal availability is represented by `maxWithdraw` and `maxRedeem`, not by reducing total NAV.

`maxDeposit`, `maxDepositForShares`, `maxWithdraw`, and `maxRedeem` MUST return `0` when the relevant user flow would currently revert because of pause state, disabled asset configuration, policy failure that can be evaluated from the supplied parameters, stale rates, deposit caps, fixed-yield shortfall pause, or insufficient synchronous withdrawal liquidity. They MAY return a conservative value lower than the true limit, but MUST NOT return a value that is known to exceed the current executable limit.

## Deposit Semantics

Deposits are asset-specific. The user names the asset they are depositing and the receiver of newly minted shares.

`deposit(asset, assets, receiver)` and `depositWithMemo(asset, assets, receiver, memo)` MUST:

1. Reject zero `assets` and `receiver == address(0)`.
2. Reject if the vault is paused.
3. Reject if `assetConfig(asset).canDeposit == false`.
4. Validate the deposit under `depositPolicyId`, including at least caller, asset, assets, and receiver.
5. Validate the share mint under `shareTransferPolicyId` and TIP-1028 receive-policy rules.
6. Read a fresh valid rate for `asset`.
7. Compute shares under the vault's configured distribution mode and fees.
8. Pull `assets` from the caller.
9. For a single-strategy vault, route the net deposit into the configured strategy adapter before minting shares.
10. Mint exactly `shares` vault-backed TIP-20 units to `receiver`.
11. Emit the ordinary TIP-20 `Transfer(address(0), receiver, shares)` event, the ordinary TIP-20 `Mint(receiver, shares)` event if still used by TIP-20 indexing, and the vault `Deposit` event.

For TIP-20 deposit assets, the vault MAY use `system_transfer_from(caller, address(this), assets)` only if the vault precompile is on the TIP-1035 Implicit Approval List. This approve-less path is allowed only for user-initiated deposits where the transfer source is the caller, or where the source has provided a non-replayable permit or account-key authorization. Managed execution MUST NOT use implicit approval to pull assets from users.

For non-TIP-20 assets, adapters MAY require ordinary ERC-20 allowance. Such adapters are not the optimized native path.

`depositForShares(asset, shares, maxAssets, receiver)` MUST follow the same authorization and policy order, but computes the asset amount needed to mint exactly `shares` and reverts if `assets > maxAssets`.

## Withdrawal Semantics

Withdrawals are asset-specific. The user names the asset they want to receive.

`withdraw(asset, assets, maxShares, receiver, owner)` MUST:

1. Reject zero `assets` and `receiver == address(0)`.
2. Reject if the vault is paused.
3. Reject if `assetConfig(asset).canWithdraw == false`.
4. Spend vault-share allowance from `owner` if `msg.sender != owner`.
5. Validate the withdrawal under `withdrawalPolicyId`, including at least caller, owner, asset, assets, and receiver.
6. Validate that `receiver` may receive the requested asset under the asset's transfer policy and TIP-1028 receive-policy rules.
7. Read a fresh valid rate for `asset`.
8. Compute required shares under the vault's configured distribution mode and fees.
9. Revert if `shares > maxShares`.
10. Verify sufficient synchronous withdrawal liquidity for `asset`.
11. For a single-strategy vault, call the strategy adapter to redeem enough strategy value into `asset`.
12. Burn exactly `shares` from `owner`.
13. Transfer `assets` to `receiver`.
14. Emit the ordinary TIP-20 `Transfer(owner, address(0), shares)` event, the ordinary TIP-20 `Burn(owner, shares)` event if still used by TIP-20 indexing, and the vault `Withdraw` event.

`withdrawWithMemo` follows the same rules and includes the memo in the vault event.

`redeem(asset, shares, minAssets, receiver, owner)` MUST follow the same rules, but burns an exact share amount and reverts if returned assets are less than `minAssets`.

Policy and quote failures MUST happen before assets or shares move. Share burns MUST happen in the same atomic state transition as asset delivery.

## Distribution Modes

The distribution mode is configured at the vault level. It is not selected per user or per withdrawal because vault shares are fungible.

### Pro-Rata

`PRO_RATA` is the default mode. All yield, loss, donations, realized strategy returns, and compounded rewards remain in NAV. Each share has the same pro-rata claim on net vault assets.

Use this mode for ordinary lending yield, RWA yield, strategy returns, and reward assets that are sold into the base asset and compounded.

### Fixed Yield

`FIXED_YIELD` gives share holders a target share-value growth rate specified by `FixedYieldConfig.annualRateBps`. New deposits mint shares at the current fixed-yield share price. Withdrawals redeem shares at the current fixed-yield share price, subject to actual backing, reserve policy, and shortfall rules.

Yield above the fixed-yield liability accrues to `excessRecipient` or through the configured performance-fee path. If actual vault backing is below the fixed-yield liability:

- the vault MAY draw from `reserve` if configured;
- the vault MUST expose the shortfall through previews;
- the vault MUST pause deposits and withdrawals if `pauseOnShortfall == true`;
- the vault MUST NOT imply a guaranteed yield unless a funded reserve or explicit external guarantor exists.

Fixed-yield accounting MUST preserve share fungibility. It cannot depend on holder identity, transfer history, or which withdrawal function a holder chooses.

Changing `DistributionMode` with nonzero supply is risky because it changes user economics. Implementations MUST either reject mode changes while `totalSupply() != 0` or define an explicit transition rule that preserves each outstanding share's claim at the moment of transition.

### Merkle Rewards

`MERKLE_REWARDS` keeps principal redemption simple and distributes arbitrary rewards through cumulative Merkle claims.

The required leaf is:

```solidity
keccak256(abi.encode(
    address(vault),
    programId,
    account,
    rewardAsset,
    cumulativeAmount
))
```

The vault stores:

```solidity
mapping(bytes32 programId => RewardProgram) rewardProgram;
mapping(bytes32 programId => mapping(address account => mapping(address rewardAsset => uint256 claimed))) claimedReward;
```

For each claim:

```solidity
claimable = cumulativeAmount - claimedReward[programId][account][rewardAsset];
```

The vault MUST verify the proof against the current active root, update `claimedReward` before transferring, and transfer only the unclaimed delta to `receiver`.

This mirrors the useful cumulative-claim property of Merkl-style distributors while adding vault and program domain separation. The native vault does not depend on Merkl. A Merkl-compatible adapter MAY claim campaign rewards as the vault and route them into pro-rata NAV, fixed-yield excess, or a native reward program.

## Fees

Vaults MUST support management fees and performance fees through `FeeConfig`.

Management fees accrue based on elapsed time and net vault assets. Performance fees accrue only on yield above a high-water mark or equivalent accounted gain. Fees MUST be visible in previews and NAV accounting before user deposits or withdrawals execute.

Fee configuration is owner-controlled. If a product wants a manager to configure fees, the owner can be a manager-controlled multisig or contract. The `manager` execution role itself MUST NOT be able to change fees.

## Single-Strategy Vaults

A `SINGLE_STRATEGY` vault routes user deposits into one configured adapter. This mode is for products like a Morpho-backed vault where there is one yield source selected at initialization and no active allocation manager.

The single-strategy adapter MUST expose:

```solidity
interface ITempoSingleStrategyAdapter {
    /// @notice Returns true if the adapter can deposit this asset.
    function supportsDepositAsset(address asset) external view returns (bool);

    /// @notice Returns true if the adapter can redeem into this asset.
    function supportsWithdrawalAsset(address asset) external view returns (bool);

    /// @notice Returns strategy NAV in the vault baseAsset.
    function totalBaseAssets() external view returns (uint256 baseAssets);

    /// @notice Previews baseAsset value received for depositing assets.
    function previewDeposit(address asset, uint256 assets) external view returns (uint256 baseAssets);

    /// @notice Previews assets returned for redeeming baseAsset value.
    function previewRedeem(address asset, uint256 baseAssets) external view returns (uint256 assets);

    /// @notice Deposits assets from the vault into the strategy.
    function deposit(address asset, uint256 assets) external returns (uint256 baseAssets);

    /// @notice Redeems strategy value into asset and sends it to receiver.
    function redeem(address asset, uint256 baseAssets, address receiver) external returns (uint256 assets);
}
```

Single-strategy vaults MUST reject both `manage` overloads. The owner MAY replace the strategy only through a future explicit migration TIP; this TIP treats `strategy` as immutable after creation.

Single-strategy vaults MUST keep `manager() == address(0)`. `setManager` MUST revert unless the vault is multi-strategy.

## Multi-Strategy Vaults and Manager Execution

A `MULTI_STRATEGY` vault does not route deposits into an immutable strategy adapter. User deposits enter the vault's native accounting and withdrawal buffers. The manager can rebalance vault-owned liquidity across venues through the `manage` interface.

The `manage` overloads are intentionally BoringVault-compatible:

```solidity
function manage(address target, bytes calldata data, uint256 value)
    external
    returns (bytes memory result);

function manage(address[] calldata targets, bytes[] calldata data, uint256[] calldata values)
    external
    returns (bytes[] memory results);
```

This lets Veda's curation layer, or a small adapter around it, plug into a native Tempo vault. Veda-style manager contracts verify strategy calls offchain or through Merkle-verified decoder/sanitizer leaves, then call `vault.manage(...)`. The native vault should expose the same execution shape, while keeping deposits, withdrawals, share accounting, policies, and fees inside Tempo.

`manage` MUST:

1. Revert unless `vaultType() == MULTI_STRATEGY`.
2. Revert unless `msg.sender == manager()`.
3. Reject `delegatecall` and `callcode` execution.
4. Execute only from the vault's own asset balances.
5. Snapshot `totalSupply()` before execution and revert unless it is unchanged after execution.
6. Reject reentrant calls into user-facing deposit, withdrawal, reward-claim, owner-configuration, policy-configuration, and `manage` methods on the same vault.
7. Reject calls that update owner, manager, asset config, rate providers, fees, distribution mode, fixed-yield config, transfer policy, reward programs, or pause state.
8. Reject any use of TIP-1035 implicit approval to pull tokens from arbitrary users.
9. Execute batch calls atomically: one failed call reverts the whole batch.
10. Emit `Managed` for each call and `BatchManaged` for batch execution.

The manager can be an EOA, multisig, contract, Veda manager contract, or Tempo adapter. It is not a separate curator role in the native vault.

## Policy Integration

Vault-backed TIP-20s use TIP-403 and TIP-1028 in three places:

1. The input asset transfer into the vault.
2. The share mint or share transfer to the receiver.
3. The output asset transfer to the withdrawal receiver.

`depositPolicyId` gates who can deposit a specific asset and who can receive shares from that deposit. `withdrawalPolicyId` gates who can redeem shares and who can receive the withdrawal asset. `shareTransferPolicyId` governs normal share transfers and share mints because vault shares are payment assets.

Policy failures MUST revert before funds or shares move. Receive-policy failures MUST follow TIP-1028 semantics for the specific token movement being attempted.

## Payment Lane and Fee AMM Integration

Because the vault address is a TIP-20 token address, vault shares can be used anywhere ordinary TIP-20 balances are accepted, including wallets, transfers, approvals, account keys, Fee AMM routing, and payment flows, subject to each system's existing token eligibility rules.

This TIP also requires a TIP-1045 payment call allow-list amendment for bounded vault deposit and withdrawal selectors if deposits and redemptions should receive payment-lane inclusion. The eligible selectors SHOULD be limited to:

- `deposit(address,uint256,address)`
- `depositWithMemo(address,uint256,address,bytes32)`
- `withdraw(address,uint256,uint256,address,address)`
- `withdrawWithMemo(address,uint256,uint256,address,address,bytes32)`
- `redeem(address,uint256,uint256,address,address)`

The `manage` selectors and Merkle proof reward selectors MUST NOT be payment-lane eligible.

## Non-Goals

This TIP does not:

- Require ERC-4626 or ERC-7575 compliance.
- Define an actively managed curation product or curator UI.
- Make Veda, Merkl, or any other external system part of the core vault trust model.
- Add a separate curator role or reward-updater role.
- Define async redemption queues.
- Define migration from ordinary TIP-20 balances into vault-backed TIP-20 balances.
- Allow direct share minting or burning outside vault accounting.

# Observability

Implementations MUST emit:

- `TokenCreated`: preserves compatibility with existing TIP-20 factory indexers.
- `VaultTokenCreated`: identifies vault-backed TIP-20s, vault type, distribution mode, owner, manager, strategy, base asset, and initial transfer policy.
- `Deposit`: answers who deposited, which asset entered, who received shares, how many assets entered, how many shares were minted, and which memo was attached.
- `Withdraw`: answers who initiated an exit, which owner burned shares, which receiver received assets, which asset left, how many assets left, how many shares were burned, and which memo was attached.
- Ordinary TIP-20 `Transfer`, `Mint`, `Burn`, approval, memo, pause, logo, and transfer-policy events as applicable for share indexing.
- `AssetConfigUpdated`: answers how asset eligibility, rate providers, caps, policies, and buffers changed.
- `FeeConfigUpdated`: answers when management or performance fee configuration changed.
- `DistributionModeUpdated` and `FixedYieldConfigUpdated`: answer how the vault's yield accounting changed.
- `ManagerUpdated`: answers who can execute multi-strategy rebalancing.
- `OwnerTransferStarted` and `OwnerUpdated`: answer ownership transition state.
- `RewardProgramUpdated`: answers which reward roots are active for native Merkle programs.
- `RewardClaimed`: answers which cumulative reward deltas were paid.
- `Managed` and `BatchManaged`: answer which manager calls moved vault-owned liquidity.

# Invariants

- The vault address and share token address are the same object.
- `ITIP20Factory.isTIP20(vault) == true` after successful creation.
- `ITIP20VaultFactory.isVaultToken(vault) == true` after successful creation.
- `totalSupply()` equals the sum of all holder share balances after every successful external call.
- Shares are fungible. Equal share balances have equal claims under the vault's configured distribution mode.
- Direct TIP-20 issuance and direct burn entrypoints revert on vault-backed TIP-20s.
- Deposits are the only user path that can increase `totalSupply()`.
- Withdrawals and redemptions are the only user paths that can decrease `totalSupply()`.
- Deposits, withdrawals, and redemptions apply policy checks before moving assets or shares.
- Share transfers apply ordinary TIP-20 policy and receive-policy behavior.
- The owner can configure the vault but cannot mint or burn shares directly.
- The manager can move vault-owned assets only through `manage` and cannot change share supply.
- `manage` cannot call back into user-facing vault methods or owner configuration on the same vault.
- `manage` cannot use TIP-1035 implicit approval to pull assets from arbitrary users.
- Single-strategy vaults reject `manage`.
- Multi-strategy vaults have no configured single-strategy adapter.
- The manager cannot configure asset prices, rate providers, policies, fees, distribution mode, or reward roots.
- Fixed-yield accounting does not depend on holder identity or transfer history.
- Cumulative Merkle reward claims can only increase `claimedReward` and can never pay more than the proven cumulative delta.
- Payment-lane eligibility applies only to bounded deposit and withdrawal selectors added to the TIP-1045 allow-list, never to `manage` or Merkle proof claims.
