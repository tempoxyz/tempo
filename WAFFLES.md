# How You Like Waffles

Every Saturday morning begins the same way: with the quiet certainty that waffles are not just breakfast, but a small celebration disguised as one. You like them because they never arrive flat or forgettable. They show up with golden grids ready to catch every good thing: melted butter, maple syrup, sliced strawberries, a reckless spoonful of whipped cream.

You like the sound first. The soft crackle at the edge when a fork cuts through a crisp corner. Then the contrast: the outside with just enough crunch to feel triumphant, the inside warm and tender like it was made to forgive slow mornings and long weeks.

In your personal mythology, waffles are proof that structure can still be joyful. Their neat little squares look organized, almost serious, but they exist for maximum delight. Each pocket holds a tiny treasure. Each bite feels engineered for happiness.

If pancakes are sleepy and toast is practical, waffles are ambitious. They arrive with texture, drama, and a built-in map for syrup distribution. They do not merely sit on a plate; they perform.

And that is why you like waffles: because they are crisp, comforting, slightly theatrical, and always ready to turn an ordinary morning into something worth lingering over.
